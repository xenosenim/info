Game Theory Optimal - GTO

Study Plan

Phase 1: Understanding the Basics

- Read Books: Start with foundational texts like "The Theory of Poker" by David Sklansky and "Applications of No-Limit Hold'em" by Matthew Janda.
- Watch Videos: Look for instructional videos on GTO concepts from reputable poker coaches.

Phase 2: Learning GTO Concepts

- Study Ranges: Use tools like Equilab or Flopzilla to analyze hand ranges and equity.
- Practice with Software: Utilize GTO solvers (e.g., PioSolver, GTO+, Simple Postflop) to study specific spots and understand optimal strategies.

Phase 3: Application and Review

- Play Regularly: Apply GTO concepts in your games, focusing on maintaining balanced ranges and appropriate bet sizing.
- Review Sessions: After each session, review hands using solvers to compare your decisions against GTO solutions.
