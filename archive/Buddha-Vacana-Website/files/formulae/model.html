Jāgariyaṃ Anuyoga
— Devotion to wakefulness —
[jāgariyā anuyoga]

The training has to be practiced keenly and without interruption day and night. This formula is almost always accompanied by Indriyesu Guttadvāratā and Bhojane Mattaññutā.

Note


1. having fixed his mind on the perception of zeal: uṭṭhāna-saññaṃ manasi karitvā Bhikkhu Bodhi rather translates: 'noting in his mind the time for rising'; and Thanissaro Bhikkhu: 'with his mind set on getting up [either as soon as he awakens or at a particular time]' or: 'reflecting on the thought of getting up again'. The present interpretation insists on the fact that the bhikkhu keeps the continuity of the practice even while lying down to sleep (as also instructed in the formula for Sampajañña), so that his mind may remain in this particular state during its rest, to avoid the arising of evil states during sleep due to lack of vigilance. However, there is no reason to cling to one or the other of these interpretations.
